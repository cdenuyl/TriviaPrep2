# Notable Sports News (April 21 - May 5, 2025)

Source: ESPN Latest News (http://www.espn.com/espn/latestnews)

**NFL:**
*   Washington D.C. named host city for the 2027 NFL Draft. (May 5)
*   Former Patriots WR Julian Edelman voted into the team's Hall of Fame by fans. (May 5)
*   Cleveland Browns LB Devin Bush arrested and charged with assault. (May 5)
*   Buffalo Bills reportedly adding free agent WR Elijah Moore. (May 5)
*   Green Bay Packers picked up the 5th-year option for DL Devonte Wyatt, declined option for LB Quay Walker. (May 5)
*   The 2025 NFL Draft took place from April 25-27, with QB Cam Ward selected first overall by the Indianapolis Colts.
*   Former agent and business partner of John Elway, Gary Zimmerman, died after a fall. (May 3)

**NBA:**
*   The second round of the 2025 NBA Playoffs is underway. Key matchups include Celtics vs. Knicks and Thunder vs. Nuggets. (May 5)
*   Utah Jazz and Head Coach Will Hardy agreed to a contract extension. (May 5)
*   Tyrese Haliburton led the Indiana Pacers to a Game 1 victory over the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Semifinals. (May 4)
*   The Golden State Warriors defeated the Houston Rockets in Game 7 of their first-round series. (May 5)
*   The Denver Nuggets defeated the Los Angeles Clippers in Game 7 of their first-round series, setting up a matchup with the Oklahoma City Thunder. (May 3)
*   Gregg Popovich announced his retirement as head coach of the San Antonio Spurs; Mitch Johnson named successor. (May 3)

**MLB:**
*   Arizona Diamondbacks unveiled new City Connect uniforms. (May 5)
*   Kansas City Royals set a club record by hitting seven home runs in a game against the Baltimore Orioles. Maikel Garcia hit two. (May 4)
*   Boston Red Sox 1B Triston Casas confirmed out for the season after knee surgery. (May 5)
*   Shohei Ohtani and Freddie Freeman homered as the Los Angeles Dodgers defeated the Atlanta Braves. Roki Sasaki earned his first MLB win. (May 5)
*   Chicago Cubs rookie Pete Crow-Armstrong homered for the third time in two games. (May 3)
*   Los Angeles Angels star Mike Trout placed on the Injured List with a bone bruise. (May 2)
*   Cincinnati Reds pitcher Hunter Greene struck out 12 batters against the Washington Nationals. (May 2)
*   San Diego Padres star Fernando Tatis Jr. left a game after being hit by a pitch; X-rays were negative. (May 2)
